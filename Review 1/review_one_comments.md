# Panel Review 1 Comments & Feedback

The following comments were received during the Panel Review 1. These points have been addressed in the documents prepared for Review 2.

## 1. Scope and Use Case
- The application scope should be clearly defined. Highlight the key challenges addressed by personalization and drift handling in time-series environments.
- Narrow down and strengthen the use case. Explain where exactly the proposed approach will be most useful in real-world scenarios rather than keeping the time-series application too generic.
- The current time-series framing is too generic; make the problem statement more specific and impactful by targeting a well-defined domain or application where the proposed method demonstrates clear effectiveness.

## 2. Research Novelty and Methodology
- Clearly articulate the research novelty, especially the role and contribution of drift detection within the framework.
- Provide stronger justification for why drift detection is necessary and how significantly it impacts performance in heterogeneous environments.

## 3. Privacy and Comparisons
- Elaborate on privacy aspects. Distinguish clearly between general privacy and differential privacy if both are being considered.
- Include a comparative discussion of federated learning methods such as FedAvg, FedPer, and FedProp, and justify the choice of the proposed approach.

## 4. Technical Depth and Motivation
- Improve the technical depth of the presentation. Each major component should include a brief technical overview explaining how it works internally.
- Strengthen the motivation by connecting the proposed system more explicitly to real-world deployment scenarios.
